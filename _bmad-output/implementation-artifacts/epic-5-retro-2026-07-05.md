## What Went Well

1. **Pattern reuse without reinvention.** The `pg_try_advisory_xact_lock` + partial-unique-index-backstop pattern (ADR-5.1-01) was designed once in 5.1 and reused verbatim, re-keyed only to the relevant resource id, in 5.2 (rotation-scoped) and 5.3 (credential-scoped, deliberately sharing 5.1's exact key domain so break-glass and normal initiation serialize against each other for free).
2. **Adversarial review applied to every story.** Epic 4's retro flagged inconsistent review coverage (2 of 4 stories) as a governance gap and proposed a team agreement to fix it. Epic 5 honored that agreement fully — every story got a review, and every critical/high finding (10 across the three reviews) was fixed pre-implementation, including a genuinely epic-breaking one: 5.3's adversarial review (CR8/ADR-5.3-08) caught that the `stale_recovery` status would let two rotations be simultaneously "active" for one credential unless the uniqueness index was widened — exactly the kind of invariant every later feature silently assumes holds.
3. **Duplication discipline compounded.** `jscpd`'s repo-wide 0%-clone gate drove real extraction in every story: `encryptValue`, `lockCredentialInProject`/`credentialExistsInProject` (5.1), a shared `withCas()` helper (5.2), and `writeRotationAuditEntry`/several worker test helpers (5.3) — the module got cleaner as the epic progressed, not messier.
4. **Full Epic 5 status vocabulary declared up front (ADR-5.1-02).** Because 5.1 declared all five `rotations` statuses and four `rotation_checklist_items` statuses in its first migration, neither 5.2 nor 5.3 needed a CHECK-widening migration — a real, paid-off bet on up-front design.

## What Didn't Go Well

1. **A response-schema serialization bug bit the team twice.** In both 5.1 and 5.2, listing the generic `ApiErrorSchema` *first* inside a `z.union([...])` caused Fastify's Zod serializer to match the generic shape first and silently strip extra fields (`rotationId`, `pendingItems`, etc.) from the wire response. Both instances were caught by TDD, not by design review — worth a house rule (see Action Items).
2. **An assumption about global MFA enforcement was wrong.** 5.1's own Dev Notes claimed MFA enforcement was "handled globally... no route-level option needed." It wasn't — `requireMfa` is opt-in per route, and several existing routes explicitly opt out. This was caught only because the adversarial review flagged the untested assumption (finding #12) before implementation; had it shipped as originally written, rotation initiation — one of the most sensitive write paths in the system — would have had no MFA gate at all.
3. **A carried-forward Epic 4 action item was only partially honored.** Epic 4's retro (P4-2) asked for four Epic-5-facing requirements to become *explicit acceptance criteria* in Story 5.1. Three of four landed as real schema/AC requirements (project_id, initiated_by, full status vocabulary). The fourth — locking the parent project row to close a TOCTOU race with Story 4.4's archive guard — was documented as a "flag it, don't do it" note in 5.1's Cross-Epic Coordination section and never actually implemented. It sat open in `deferred-work.md` from 2026-07-01 until this retrospective surfaced it again.
4. **A Product Surface Contract decision was left pending until retro time.** Story 5.1 explicitly said the rotation-workflow web UI question ("does a web story already exist, or do we need to add one to deferred-work.md") must be resolved before `epic-5-retrospective` runs. It sat as an open "TBD" for the entire epic instead of being resolved at design time.

## Significant Discovery Alert — Risk / Gap / Contradiction / Technical-Debt Audit

At Nestor's explicit request, all medium/low adversarial-review findings that were "left as-is, not blocking" across all three stories were re-examined against what actually shipped (not just the review docs). Twelve real, still-open findings were identified; together with the carried-forward Epic 4 TOCTOU gap, all thirteen are now consolidated into **Story 5.5** (`5-5-epic-5-completion-rotation-hardening-and-technical-debt.md`, `ready-for-dev`) rather than tracked as loose background tasks. None were fixed as part of this retrospective itself.

**Security & correctness:**
- Single self-attestation (a `member` confirming a checklist item) is the only gate before an admin's irreversible version retirement — no independent verification. (Story 5.5, AC-2)
- The highest-risk code change in the epic (`revealCurrentValue()`/`listVersionHistory()` modifications, 5.3 AC-13/AC-14) shipped with no staged rollout — worth a safety net before Epic 5 is ever deployed. (Story 5.5, AC-3)
- No idempotency protection on break-glass — a double-submit during a panicked incident creates two independent rotations silently. (Story 5.5, AC-4)
- `initiatedBy` going `NULL` (deleted user) breaks the stale-rotation direct-notification call with no documented fallback. (Story 5.5, AC-5)

**Reliability & scalability:**
- A single malformed `rotationSchedule` cron string can throw and take down the entire dashboard aggregation response. (Story 5.5, AC-6)
- Org-dashboard upcoming-rotations computation is an uncapped per-request scan across every credential in the org. (Story 5.5, AC-7)
- Stale-detection job's index has no `org_id` leading column despite per-org iteration — re-walks the same global index once per org, every cycle. (Story 5.5, AC-8)
- Background-job audit-write failure handling is unspecified — unclear whether a mid-job failure rolls back cleanly or silently aborts other orgs' processing. (Story 5.5, AC-9)

**Internal inconsistencies:**
- `abandon`'s state transition has no optimistic-lock CAS check, unlike every sibling transition in the epic. (Story 5.5, AC-10)
- Break-glass supersession's audit-payload shape was left as "implementer's choice, document which" in the original spec — needs confirming and pinning down. (Story 5.5, AC-11)
- Fail-closed audit rollback tests exist for `confirm`/`complete` but not `fail`/`retry`/`max_retries_exceeded`, despite all five claiming the identical guarantee. (Story 5.5, AC-12)
- `rotation.completed`'s audit payload omits the retired version IDs, forcing a manual join for any future incident review. (Story 5.5, AC-13)

**Already-accepted, lower-urgency debt (not part of Story 5.5 — deliberately documented trade-offs):** rotation-history cascade-delete tension with FR23 "permanently recorded" (latent, no hard-delete route exists); sealed-vault blocking the two read-only rotation GET routes; no ceiling on dependency count per credential; break-glass/stale-recovery alerts routing to owners-only by default (identical to every other alert type); rotation-scoped (not item-scoped) locking creating `409`s during concurrent legitimate multi-item confirmation.

**Process note:** the Fastify/Zod union-ordering bug (P5-1 above) was independently rediscovered in both 5.1 and 5.2 before the team internalized it — worth a wider, one-time codebase audit for the same anti-pattern in modules outside rotation, since it's proven to recur silently.

## Team Agreements (carried forward + new)

1. (Carried from Epic 4) Cross-epic requirements written in one story's Dev Notes for a future story must become that future story's explicit acceptance criteria.
2. (Carried from Epic 4, now proven) A story touching authorization, session lifecycle, or destructive/irreversible state changes gets a formal adversarial review — applied consistently, not selectively. Epic 5 is the proof this works.
3. (New) A "flag it, don't do it" deferral inside a story's Dev Notes must be paired with a tracked backlog entry, not just a `deferred-work.md` paragraph — otherwise it silently expires.
4. (New) List the generic error-response schema last in any Zod response union.
