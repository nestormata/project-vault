## Executive Summary

Epic 11 shipped one story: `11-1-branding-visual-identity`, which wired a favicon/apple-touch-icon,
added `brand-50/100/500/600/700` Tailwind `@theme` tokens, rendered the product logo in the app shell
header and the auth layout, and replaced `slate-950` with `brand-600` on the wordmark, primary nav
active-tab, and auth-flow CTAs/cross-links across 12 files. Like Epic 10, this is a mid-stream addition
with no section in the original PRD/`epics.md` — it was registered 2026-07-11 for "app logo/icon
integration and a minimal brand accent color, not owned by any existing feature epic."

`11-1`'s own adversarial code review (2026-07-11, 3-layer Blind Hunter/Edge Case Hunter/Acceptance
Auditor pass) returned zero decision-needed/patch findings and independently reverified all 10 ACs
against live file contents — a clean bar by this project's own review standard. It logged 6 non-blocking
deferred items in `deferred-work.md`, all still open and, on direct re-verification this session, all
still accurate. The story explicitly scoped its rebrand to 3 named surfaces (app shell, primary nav,
auth flow) and documented — not hid — the fact that `bg-slate-950` primary-CTA styling remains on
several other surfaces outside that scope, leaving the app visually split between a violet header/auth
experience and near-black CTAs elsewhere until a deliberate follow-up decides whether to extend the
rebrand further.

**No blocking issues were found.** The 6 deferred items are all genuine, named, low-severity technical
debt — component duplication, a missing hover state, an intentionally-scoped rebrand boundary, and a
test-coverage gap on the new brand markup — none of which represents an inconsistency or regression
that should hold up closure. Epic 11 is recommended for closure.
